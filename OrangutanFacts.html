Interesting Orangutan Facts

Orangutans are fascinating, intelligent creatures that roam around the rain forests of the Southeast Asian islands of Borneo and Sumatra. They are very recognizable because of they're distinctive red fur and long, powerful arms

That being said, here are some orangutan facts that will educate you further about Orangutans

    In the wild, a female orangutan gives birth once every eight years

    On average, female orangutans are 45 inches in height and weigh about 81.5 pounds. male orangutans average 54 inches in height and 191 pounds.

    Orangutans are the largest tree living mammal in the world

    Juvenile orangutans love playing "play fight" in the trees without showing any fear of heights

    Adult orangutans are as strong as seven people combined and can lift 500lb (240kg) effortlessly

    Orangutans are noisy creatures when they want to be, making loud howls and bellows that can be heard for miles around

    Orangutans are closely related to humans, having 97% of DNA in common.

    When on the ground, they walk on all fours, using their palms or their fists.

    Newborns have pink faces that change to dark brown or black as they age

    Orangutans are very smart. They use tools, such as sticks, to scratch their backs, get insects out of trees or break open nuts

    there are somewhere between 55,000 and 65,000 wild orangutans left.
